<p><p>The Random Access Memory (RAM) market primarily comprises DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory) and ROM (Read-Only Memory). DRAM is commonly used in devices like computers and smartphones due to its ability to store data temporarily while the device is powered on, offering fast access and high capacity. In contrast, ROM stores permanent data necessary for booting and operating systems, ensuring essential information remains intact even when the device is off. Together, they fulfill distinct yet critical roles in computing.</p></p>
