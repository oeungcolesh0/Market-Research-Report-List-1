<p><p>Present weather sensors detect atmospheric conditions to provide real-time data on weather phenomena. The market encompasses two primary types: optical and ultrasonic sensors. Optical sensors utilize light reflections to identify precipitation types and intensity, effectively sensing rain, snow, or fog. Ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves to measure droplet size and velocity, allowing for accurate detection of weather conditions, including mist and drizzle. Both technologies play a crucial role in meteorology, aviation, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
